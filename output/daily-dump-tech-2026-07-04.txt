It's July 04. This is your Daily Dump of tech news.

We're starting with a significant security alert for Linux users. A newly disclosed kernel flaw, CVE-2026-46242, nicknamed Bad Epoll, allows an ordinary user to escalate their access to full root. This impacts Linux desktops, servers, and Android devices. A fix has been released. This flaw is noteworthy because it resides in the same kernel code area where previous high-profile vulnerabilities were found, highlighting a persistent security challenge within that specific component. For engineers, this implies a need for deeper scrutiny of that kernel subsystem.

Moving to network security, Google has announced a significant disruption to NetNut, one of the largest residential proxy networks. NetNut turned two million home devices into rented relays, often without owners' knowledge. Google's Threat Intelligence Group, with the FBI and Lumen, substantially reduced NetNut's usable device pool. This collective action strikes a major blow against infrastructure commonly abused for cybercrime like credential stuffing, price scraping, and account takeovers. By routing traffic through these proxies, attackers evade IP-based blocking. The disruption significantly hampers criminals' ability to leverage this infrastructure.

In Apple news, the upcoming iOS 27 release will introduce a new framework to help applications detect when a user might be getting scammed in real time. This framework aims to combat social engineering attempts across voice calls, text messages, and emails. The technical implication is deeper integration of system-level intelligence to identify suspicious patterns, such as common scam phrases or unusual caller behavior. The framework would alert users to potential threats, adding a proactive layer of defense beyond simple spam filtering.

Over at Meta, the company has quietly launched Pocket, an experimental AI application focused on gaming. Pocket allows users to generate and share interactive mini-games using text prompts. This signals Meta's continued exploration of generative AI for specialized content creation in casual gaming. The "vibe-coded" aspect suggests an emphasis on creating games that match a particular aesthetic or mood, lowering the barrier to entry for game creation.

The US version is expected to incorporate a Qualcomm modem, while other models will use a C2 modem. This dual-sourcing strategy suggests Apple is diversifying its supply chain to mitigate risks and optimize for regional network specificities, as different cellular networks employ varying technologies and regulatory requirements.
