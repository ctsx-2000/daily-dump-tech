NASA has launched an emergency mission to prevent the Swift Observatory from crashing to Earth. Launched in 2004, Swift has been a cornerstone for gamma-ray burst astronomy. Unexpected solar storms have increased atmospheric density, causing greater drag and pushing its orbit lower much faster than anticipated. It’s now in danger of burning up as early as this year. NASA has partnered with Katalyst Space Technologies, which specializes in satellite servicing. Their Link spacecraft launched on Friday, tasked with grappling Swift and boosting it into a higher, safer orbit. This mission is critical not only for saving the observatory but also for demonstrating new on-orbit servicing capabilities, potentially revolutionizing how we manage orbital infrastructure and reducing space debris.

Moving on to AI, Midjourney is seeking to compel Hollywood studios to reveal details of their AI usage as part of an ongoing legal dispute. Midjourney faces a lawsuit from three major studios alleging copyright infringement in its AI model training. The studios claim Midjourney's text-to-image models were trained on copyrighted material without permission. By demanding transparency on the studios' own AI practices, Midjourney aims to establish a precedent regarding widespread AI use in entertainment. If studios use AI in content creation, potentially leveraging models also trained on copyrighted works, their claims against Midjourney might be seen as less straightforward. This maneuver highlights the increasing tension and lack of clear regulatory frameworks surrounding AI's role in creative fields.

In development news, Armin reports on a strange problem encountered while hacking on Pi, noting "Better Models: Worse Tools." Newer Claude models, even Opus 4.8, are sometimes calling Pi's edit tool with extra, invented fields within the nested edits array. While the core edit itself is usually correct, these spurious fields indicate unexpected behavior in the AI's interaction with external tools. It suggests a potential gap in how models handle structured outputs and API calls. Even as models become more capable, their ability to strictly adhere to tool schemas without hallucinating extra, non-existent parameters appears to be an ongoing challenge, especially with nested data structures. This can lead to unexpected errors in tool execution.

Also today, the sqlite-utils 4.0 release candidate 2 is out, with much of the development credited to Claude Fable. The creator, who previously detailed working on release candidate 1 with the AI, noted that with Claude Fable on Max subscriptions for only a few more days, he decided to push for a stable 4.0 release. This version adheres to SemVer, meaning changes between major versions are expected to introduce backward incompatibilities. The creator specifically mentioned the cost of using the AI for this development push, reportedly about $149.25 in AI compute. This demonstrates a practical application of large language models in accelerating open-source development.

Switching gears to corporate AI policies, Alibaba has reportedly classified Claude Code as high-risk software and banned its employees from using it. This internal policy signals growing concerns among major corporations about security implications and potential data leakage risks associated with employees using third-party AI coding assistants. Tools like Claude Code, while powerful, often require sending proprietary code or sensitive data to the AI provider's servers. This creates a significant risk for intellectual property theft or compliance breaches. It underscores the ongoing challenge for companies to balance the productivity benefits of advanced AI tools with enterprise-level security and compliance requirements.

In Apple news, the upcoming A20 Pro chip for the iPhone 18 Pro and Pro Max is reportedly set to break a thirteen-year convention by adopting ninety-six-bit LPDDR6 memory. For over a decade, Apple has used a sixty-four-bit memory interface for its iPhone chips. The move to ninety-six-bit LPDDR6 marks a significant increase in memory bandwidth, crucial for supporting demanding AI and computational workloads on-device. However, the report also suggests Apple might be cutting costs on the iPhone 18 Pro duo's NAND storage. This could mean a slower type of NAND or smaller base storage configurations, potentially offsetting the increased cost of the advanced memory architecture.

Finally, the Dune keypad device has been released, designed to function as a versatile meeting controller and more. This gadget features three programmable buttons, and its core innovation lies in its context-aware functionality. This means the device intelligently changes its actions based on the application currently in focus. For example, in meeting applications, buttons can dynamically reconfigure to perform tasks like toggling mic mute, video on/off, and bringing the meeting window to the front. This eliminates navigating menus or remembering shortcuts, streamlining common tasks. Beyond meetings, the same context-aware principle could apply to other applications, offering tailored controls for creative suites or development environments.
