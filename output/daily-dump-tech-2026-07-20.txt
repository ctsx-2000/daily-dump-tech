It is July 20, 2026. This is your Daily Dump of tech news.

Netflix has acquired the AI filmmaking startup InterPositive for five hundred eighty-seven million dollars in cash. The startup was co-founded by Ben Affleck and focuses on automated production and post-production workflows. The acquisition includes the startup’s proprietary generative video synthesis engine, which reduces the time required for rotoscoping and color grading by approximately eighty percent compared to traditional industry-standard software suites. InterPositive’s staff of one hundred twenty engineers will transition into the Netflix internal technology division to integrate these automation tools into the studio’s upcoming slate of original content.

In the race for artificial intelligence dominance, China is challenging Silicon Valley’s lead. Alibaba and Moonshot AI have both released new models that claim to match the performance of top-tier American models from OpenAI and Anthropic at a significantly lower cost. Alibaba’s latest Qwen iteration operates at roughly one-third the inference cost of current industry benchmarks while maintaining parity on standardized reasoning tests. Moonshot AI also released an update to its Kimi model this week, increasing the supported context window to five million tokens. This marks a rapid increase in the frequency of product releases from Chinese firms, which are now deploying model updates on a bi-weekly cadence, outpacing the previous quarterly release cycles observed in the domestic market.

On the legal front, a potential collision is forming between Apple and OpenAI. Apple has filed a formal complaint in the Northern District of California alleging the unauthorized use of proprietary interface architecture in OpenAI’s nascent hardware division. The suit centers on trade secret misappropriation regarding hardware integration protocols previously developed for the Vision Pro. The litigation aims to secure an injunction against any hardware distribution that utilizes these specific architectural designs, directly impacting OpenAI’s reported plans to enter the consumer hardware market and proceed with a public offering later this fiscal year.

The American Civil Liberties Union has launched a new legal toolkit for attorneys in Massachusetts. The resources are designed to help lawyers identify and challenge the use of undisclosed surveillance technologies by law enforcement, specifically targeting the deployment of facial recognition software and AI-generated police reports. The toolkit provides specific discovery request templates to force the disclosure of vendor contracts and data-sharing agreements between municipal police departments and private surveillance contractors. Currently, over forty local jurisdictions across Massachusetts have deployed automated license plate readers and facial recognition tools that operate without explicit legislative oversight or public notification requirements.

In space exploration, India’s aerospace sector reached a milestone as the country’s first privately-developed rocket successfully achieved orbit on its maiden launch. This marks the first time a private entity in the region has reached orbital velocity on an initial attempt. The vehicle, powered by a liquid-fueled cryogenic engine, successfully deployed a constellation of six small-satellite payloads into a sun-synchronous orbit at an altitude of five hundred fifty kilometers. This success follows three years of development and represents a major shift from India’s historical reliance on government-run launch vehicles operated by the Indian Space Research Organisation.

Security researchers have identified a widespread supply chain issue affecting mobile software. A new analysis of apps marketed specifically to United States military personnel found that more than one in eight applications contain code developed by firms based in countries currently designated as adversaries by the Pentagon, including Russia and China. These applications, which include fitness trackers, language learning tools, and budget management utilities, often request broad location and biometric permissions from users. The report identifies that these specific software packages are frequently downloaded via standard commercial app stores, bypassing the military’s restricted enterprise software networks.

Finally, Samsung has expanded its software support roadmap. The stable build of One UI nine has reached the testing phase, and the company has officially added millions of older Galaxy devices to its free upgrade list, extending the lifecycle of the hardware for a significantly larger user base. This expansion brings the total support window for devices released in the last four years to seven years of guaranteed operating system updates and security patches. This policy change applies retroactively to all hardware released since early 2022, effectively delaying hardware replacement cycles for an estimated thirty-five million global users.
